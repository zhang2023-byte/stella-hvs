**DeepXiv brief**

[research paper] High-resolution $N$-body simulations of massive black hole binaries (MBHBs) in spherical and rotating galactic nuclei reveal that stellar rotation significantly alters the phase-space distribution of ejected stars and induces strong eccentricity growth via slingshot interactions. The angle between the MBHB orbital plane and the stellar system's rotation plane controls the scattering dynamics, with massive MBHBs actively converting counter-rotating stars into co-rotating ones, thereby enhancing hardening efficiency. Results show large statistical fluctuations beyond semi-analytic models, demonstrating that rotation-induced orbital mixing and asymmetric scattering are critical for resolving the Final Parsec Problem in gas-poor galaxy mergers.

**Search Abstract**

The hierarchical galaxy formation picture suggests that super massive black holes (MBHs) observed in galactic nuclei today have grown from coalescence of massive black hole binaries (MBHB) after galaxy merging. Once the components of a MBHB become gravitationally bound, strong three-body encounters between the MBHB and stars dominate its evolution in a "dry" gas free environment, and change the MBHB's energy and angular momentum (semi-major axis, eccentricity and orientation). Here we present high accuracy direct N-body simulations of spherical and axisymmetric (rotating) galactic nuclei with order a million stars and two massive black holes that are initially unbound. We analyze the properties of the ejected stars due to slingshot effects from three-body encounters with the MBHB in detail. Previous studies have investigated the eccentricity and energy changes of MBHs using approximate models or Monte-Carlo three body scatterings. We find general agreement with the average results of previous semi-analytic models for spherical galactic nuclei, but our results show a large statistical variation. Our new results show many more phase space details of how the process works, and also show the influence of stellar system rotation on the process. We detect that the angle between the orbital plane of the MBHBs and that of the stellar system (when it rotates) influences the phase-space properties of the ejected stars. We also find that massive MBHB tend to switch stars with counter-rotating orbits into co-rotating orbits during their interactions.
