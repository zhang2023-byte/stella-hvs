- DeepXiv keywords：hypervelocity star dynamics, supermassive black hole ejection, LMC orbital boosting, proper motion trajectory reconstruction, anisotropic stellar clustering

**DeepXiv brief**

[research paper] The authors reanalyze the trajectories of 21 hypervelocity B-type stars (HVSs) using Gaia DR3 proper motions and updated Milky Way–Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC) orbital constraints, finding that half of the stars trace back to the LMC rather than the Galactic Center. They construct a forward model of HVS ejection from an LMC supermassive black hole (SMBH), showing that the observed angular clustering—specifically the 'Leo Overdensity'—arises due to orbital boosting of LMC-origin HVSs by ~300 km/s, which preferentially selects stars aligned with the LMC's motion. This SMBH-driven mechanism rules out supernova runaways and dynamical ejections, and yields a mass constraint of $10^{5.8_{-0.4}^{+0.2}}M_{igodot}$ ($acksimeq 6	imes10^5M_{igodot}$) for the LMC SMBH.

**摘要**

Hypervelocity stars (HVSs) are produced by the Hills mechanism when a stellar binary is disrupted by a supermassive black hole (SMBH). The HVS Survey detected 21 unbound B-type main-sequence stars in the Milky Way's outer halo that are consistent with ejection via the Hills mechanism. We revisit the trajectories of these stars in light of proper motions from {\\\\it Gaia} DR3 and modern constraints on the Milky Way -- Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC) orbit. We find that half of the unbound HVSs discovered by the HVS Survey trace back not the Galactic Center, but to the LMC. Motivated by this finding, we construct a forward-model for HVSs ejected from an SMBH in the LMC and observed through the selection function of the HVS Survey. The predicted spatial and kinematic distributions of simulated HVSs are remarkably similar to the observed distributions. In particular, we reproduce the conspicuous angular clustering of HVSs around the constellation Leo. This clustering occurs because HVSs from the LMC are boosted by $\\\\sim300\\\\,{\\\\rm km\\\\,s^{-1}}$ by the orbital motion of the LMC, and stars launched parallel to this motion are preferentially selected as HVS candidates. We find that the birth rate and clustering of LMC HVSs cannot be explained by supernova runaways or dynamical ejection scenarios not involving a SMBH. From the ejection velocities and relative number of Magellanic vs. Galactic HVSs, we constrain the mass of the LMC SMBH to be $10^{5.8^{+0.2}_{-0.4}} M_{\\\\odot}$ ($\\\\simeq 6\\\\times10^5 M_{\\\\odot}$).

**DeepXiv brief**

[theoretical astrophysics research paper] The paper models tidal disruption events (TDEs) of cold, planetary-mass 'snow clouds'—hypothetical molecular clouds where hydrogen condenses into solid H₂—by unassociated field stars using an affine hydrodynamic formalism. Simulations reveal that debris streams form as over-pressured, microstructured plasma with cold helium and meter-sized H₂ snowballs that erode via shock heating in the interstellar medium, producing plasma filaments resembling radio scintillation 'scattering screens'. The predicted disruption rate is too low to explain observed scattering screens under the assumption of random encounters, suggesting that snow clouds are either rare or not the dominant source of such phenomena.

**摘要**

It has been suggested that star-forming galaxies may host a substantial, dark reservoir of gas in the form of planetary-mass molecular clouds that are so cold that $\\\\text{H}_{2}$ can condense. Here we investigate the process of tidal disruption of such "snow clouds" by close passage of field stars. We construct a suite of simulations using the hydrodynamic formalism introduced by Carter and Luminet, and use it to explore the properties of the resulting tidal debris. The debris streams are tiny structures that are highly over-pressured relative to the ambient interstellar medium (ISM). They are also unusual in their composition--initially consisting of cold, gaseous He together with $\\\\text{H}_{2}$ "snowballs" that may be as much as a metre in size. Each stream expands and cools and is subsequently shocked as it plows through the ISM; the snowballs are gradually eroded by the shocked gas. Snowballs streaming through the shocked ISM create microstructured plasma that is somewhat reminiscent of the "scattering screens" revealed by radio-wave scintillation studies. However, the tidal disruption rate is too low to account for the observed number of scattering screens if, as we assume here, the stars and clouds have no prior physical association so that disruptions occur as a result of chance encounters between stars and clouds.

**摘要**

We provide the first reference framework for extremely metal-poor (XMP) OB-type stars. By using an extensive grid of models, we account for the different combinations of stellar parameters that result in the same spectral morphology and provide a range of parameters and colours compatible with each spectral subtype and luminosity class. We supply updated photometric criteria to optimize candidate selection of OB stars in XMP environments. We find 0.10 $Z_{\\\\odot}$ OB stars are 1-6 kK hotter and produce higher ionizing fluxes than their Galactic analogues. Finally, we used our calibrated colours to map the extinction of the 0.10 $Z_{\\\\odot}$ galaxy Sextans A finding that reddening is non-negligible and uneven.

### 5. Enabling high mass accretion rates onto massive main sequence stars by outer envelope mass removal

- 发布时间：2025-01-07T00:00:00
- 作者：Ariel Scolnic Ealeal Bear Noam Soker
- 分类：astro-ph.SR, astro-ph.HE
- 引用数：2
- DeepXiv score：15.492803
- 命中关键词：`high velocity stars`, `high-velocity stars`
- 命中分类：`astro-ph.SR`
- 标题复核：mass removal mechanisms，confidence=0.8，Mass removal from massive stars could relate to runaway ejection mechanisms
- DeepXiv keywords：jet-driven mass removal, accretion disk dynamics, massive star evolution, common envelope evolution, stellar expansion suppression

**DeepXiv brief**

[research paper] A one-dimensional MESA simulation demonstrates that massive main-sequence stars (30–80 M⊙) can accrete up to ∼10% of their mass at high rates without significant expansion by coupling accretion with simultaneous jet-driven mass removal from the outer envelope. The model alternates mass addition (via accretion disk) and mass removal (via jets), maintaining a deep potential well through a dense accretion disk that launches jets within the inflated envelope, enabling sustained high accretion rates consistent with luminous red novae and the Great Eruption of Eta Carinae.

**摘要**

Using the one-dimensional numerical code MESA, we simulate mass accretion at very high rates onto massive main sequence stars, M=30, 60, 80 Mo, and find that these stars can accrete up to 10% of their mass without expanding much if we consider a simultaneous mass removal by jets. In this jetted-mass-removal accretion scenario, the accretion is through an accretion disk that launches jets. When the star expands due to rapid mass accretion, it engulfs the inner zones of the accretion disk and the jets it launches. We assume that these jets remove the outer layers of the envelope. We mimic this in the one-dimensional numerical code by alternating mass addition and mass removal parts. We add mass and energy, the accretion energy, to the outer layers of the envelope, leading to rapid stellar expansion. When the star expands by a few tens of percent, we stop mass addition and start mass removal until the star returns to its initial radius. We also show that the density of the accretion disk is larger than the density of the outer layers of the inflated envelope, allowing the disk to launch jets inside the outer inflated envelope layers. Our results show that main sequence stars can accrete mass at high rates while maintaining the deep potential well, as some models of eruptive systems require, e.g., some luminous red novae, the grazing envelope evolution, and the 1837-1856 Great Eruption of Eta Carinae.

### 6. Little Red Dots are Tidal Disruption Events in Runaway-Collapsing Clusters

- arXiv：[2501.03309](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.03309)；[PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.03309)
- 发布时间：2025-01-06T00:00:00
- 作者：Jillian Bellovary
- 分类：astro-ph.GA, astro-ph.HE
- 引用数：10
- DeepXiv score：18.333773
- 命中关键词：`runaway stars`, `OB runaway stars`
- 命中分类：`astro-ph.GA`
- 标题复核：Runaway clusters/TDEs，confidence=0.8，Title mentions 'Runaway-Collapsing Clusters' which suggests runaway stellar dynamics
- DeepXiv keywords：runaway collapse, tidal disruption events, intermediate-mass black holes, high-redshift galaxy formation, stellar cluster dynamics

**DeepXiv brief**

[Theoretical astrophysics letter] Proposes that Little Red Dots (LRDs) at high redshift are not star-forming galaxies or active galactic nuclei, but instead tidal disruption events (TDEs) in dense, runaway-collapsing stellar clusters that host intermediate-mass black hole seeds. These clusters form via dynamical instability in massive, metal-poor star clusters, leading to recurrent TDEs that produce compact, UV-bright systems with broad Hα emission but no X-ray or far-IR emission—matching LRD observations. The predicted number density of such clusters is 4 orders of magnitude higher than observed LRD counts, suggesting either a missing physical component (e.g., a top-heavy initial mass function) or that TDEs are not the dominant emission mechanism, though the hypothesis remains viable with follow-up variability observations.

**摘要**

I hypothesize a physical explanation for the "Little Red Dots" (LRDs) discovered by the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST). The first star formation in the universe occurs in dense clusters, some of which may undergo runaway collapse and form an intermediate mass black hole. This process would appear as a very dense stellar system, with recurring tidal disruption events (TDEs) as stellar material is accreted by the black hole. Such a system would be compact, UV-emitting, and exhibit broad H-alpha emission. If runaway collapse is the primary mechanism for forming massive black hole seeds, this process could be fairly common and explain the large volume densities of LRDs. In order to match the predicted number density of runaway collapse clusters, the tidal disruption rate must be on the order of 10^-4 per year. A top-heavy stellar initial mass function may be required to match observations without exceeding the predicted LambdaCDM mass function. The TDE LRD hypothesis can be verified with followup JWST observations looking for TDE-like variability.
