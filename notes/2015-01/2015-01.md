**DeepXiv brief**

[astronomical research paper] The authors identify four low-mass hypervelocity star (HVS) candidates using high-precision proper motion data from the SDSS Stripe 82 survey, combined with spectroscopic radial velocity measurements from the Hale Telescope. Due to lack of metallicity data for K/M-type stars, distances and total velocities are estimated under a conservative [Fe/H] = -2 dex assumption, yielding total velocities exceeding 400 km/s for the best candidates; one candidate (NHCa) shows a high likelihood of being unbound from the Milky Way across a wide range of metallicities, suggesting a possible origin via tidal disruption by the galactic center.

**Search Abstract**

Hypervelocity stars are those that have speeds exceeding the escape speed and are hence unbound from the Milky Way. We investigate a sample of low-mass hypervelocity candidates obtained using data from the high-precision SDSS Stripe 82 catalogue, which we have combined with spectroscopy from the 200-inch Hale Telescope at Palomar Observatory. We find four good candidates, but without metallicities it is difficult to pin-down their distances and therefore total velocities. Our best candidate has a significant likelihood that it is escaping the Milky Way for a wide-range of metallicities.
