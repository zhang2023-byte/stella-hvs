**DeepXiv brief**

[astronomical reclassification study] The paper reclassifies the previously reported bow-shock nebula associated with runaway star HD 185806 as the known planetary nebula WHTZ 1 (Ra 7), using multi-wavelength imaging and spectroscopy to demonstrate that the emission arises from a high-excitation planetary nebula with a blue ionizing central star, not from a stellar wind shock. The reassignment is supported by historical records, morphological consistency, spectral features, and archival data, resolving a misattribution that had persisted in recent literature.

**Search Abstract**

We present evidence that the nebular cocoon and bow-shock emission nebula putatively and recently reported as deriving from the 9th magnitude "runaway" star HD 185806 is the previously discovered but obscure planetary nebula WHTZ 1 (Ra 7). It has a Gaia DR3 G~16 blue ionizing star at its geometric centre. We present imagery, spectroscopy, other data and arguments to support that this emission source is a high excitation Planetary Nebula not a stellar wind bow shock.
