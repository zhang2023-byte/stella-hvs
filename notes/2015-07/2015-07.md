**DeepXiv brief**

[research paper] The paper presents a 3-body dynamical simulation framework to investigate the ejection of stars from globular clusters (GCs) during close encounters with supermassive black holes (SMBHs), showing that GCs with high mass and eccentric orbits can produce high-velocity stars (HVS) via gravitational scattering. The branching ratios of ejected stars, S-stars (BH-bound), and retained stars depend on GC mass, orbital eccentricity, and galactic potential structure, with higher GC masses increasing the fraction of unbound HVS in spiral galaxies due to stronger escape velocities. Results demonstrate that SMBH-GC interactions can be a significant source of HVS, particularly in galaxies with extended dark matter halos.

**Search Abstract**

The origin of high velocity stars observed in the halo of our Galaxy is still unclear. In this work we test the hypothesis, raised by results of recent high precision $N$-body simulations, of strong acceleration of stars belonging to a massive globular cluster orbitally decayed in the central region of the host galaxy where it suffers of a close interaction with a super massive black hole, which, for these test cases, we assumed $10^8$ M$_\\\\odot$ in mass.
