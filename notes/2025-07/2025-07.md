There are two main mechanisms for the formation of runaway stars, i.e., the binary-supernova scenario (BSS) and the dynamical ejection scenario (DES). **DeepXiv brief**

[research paper] A forensic dynamical reconstruction reveals that the ejection of the massive binary Mel 34 from the R136 cluster 52 kyr ago required a five-star encounter involving a tight triple system (Mel 39–VFTS 590 binary) and the binary Mel 34, defying prior expectations that runaways arise from binary supernovae or two-body interactions. The interaction's deterministic Newtonian dynamics allow precise backtracking of velocities and trajectories, predicting that Mel 39 (an 80 M⊙ binary) will escape at ~64 km/s, while all five stars will explode as supernovae in 5 Myr, forming black hole binaries that are unlikely to merge within a Hubble time. This work establishes a rare case of multi-body dynamical ejection in a dense stellar environment, with implications for runaway star formation and cluster evolution.
